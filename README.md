# Amplifiers at the Boundary

**A project on the epistemology of near-critical sensing**

U. Warring · Physikalisches Institut, Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg · 2026

---

## What this project is

This project examines atmospheric instruments from the 15th to the 19th century through an epistemological lens, asking not *when* each device was invented but *what kind of knowledge* it produces. It identifies a distinction between **calibrated state instruments** (invertible) and **sentinel instruments** (non-invertible, nonlinear, transition-sensitive), and traces the implications of that distinction from 19th-century meteorology to modern critical-transition theory.

## Two documents

The project consists of two complementary texts:

### Historical Dossier: *From Natural Signs to Measurement Networks* (v0.1.3, frozen)

A comprehensive historical survey of atmospheric instruments organised by epistemological function across six analytic layers: state, transport, integration, radiation, electrical, and sentinel. Includes primary-source citations, historiographic references, and a detailed timeline.

- **Role:** Coastline document (maps the historical territory)
- **Register:** Documentary-historical with interpretive synthesis
- **Length:** ~12,000 words
- **Status:** Source-hardened, editorially tightened, frozen

### Essay: *Amplifiers at the Boundary* (v0.2.1, circulation-ready)

An interpretive essay arguing that the sentinel tradition was displaced because its devices were non-invertible, and that the question sentinels were trying to answer — *how close is a system to a qualitative change?* — is now central to modern science. Connects storm-glass physics to bifurcation theory, critical slowing down, and early warning signals.

- **Role:** Sail document (interprets and argues)
- **Register:** Hybrid — essayistic openings/closings, expository backbone
- **Length:** ~5,500 words
- **Status:** Polished, circulation-ready

## How the two documents differ

The **dossier** is documentary. It establishes facts, dates, attributions, and sources. Its categories are explicitly marked as analytic rather than actor-native.

The **essay** is argumentative. It takes the sentinel concept from the dossier and develops it into a physics argument. It is self-contained but references the dossier as a companion.
